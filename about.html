About This Project

Istanbul: A Stage of Espionage
and Global Conflict

A web-based cinematic tourism experience built as a final project for the Master's Degree in Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge, University of Bologna. The project explores Istanbul's role as a recurring setting for spy films, action thrillers, and mysteries across six decades of cinema.

01

Project Overview

This exhibition presents Istanbul not merely as a backdrop for international cinema, but as an active participant — a city whose geography, history, and cultural identity have shaped some of the most memorable sequences in spy and thriller filmmaking from 1963 to 2016.

Rather than presenting a conventional film database or tourist guide, the project structures its content around two distinct narratives — interpretive frameworks through which the same locations can be experienced differently. The first, Espionage & Pursuit, follows a dramatic, action-driven arc through eight key locations. The second, Istanbul Through Time in Cinema, takes an analytical approach, comparing how the same sites have been filmed and framed across six decades.

The goal is to support both pre-visit planning (at home, using the interactive map and narrative explorer) and on-site exploration (following the narrative sequence through real Istanbul streets and monuments).

02

Selected Films

Five films were selected for their significant use of real Istanbul locations, their span across the 1963–2016 period, and their representation of different genres and approaches to the city's cinematic potential.

03

The Two Narratives

◈ Espionage & Pursuit

An action-driven, immersive journey structured around four chapters: Surveillance → Escape → Hideouts → Confrontation. Eight locations are visited in sequence, each presented with scene-specific narrative text and espionage tradecraft notes. The experience is designed to feel like being inside the films — following the same routes, using the same geography.

Films: From Russia with Love · Topkapi · Taken 2 · Skyfall · Inferno

◇ Istanbul Through Time

An analytical, comparative journey through seven locations organized by era: 1960s → 2012 → 2016. The same locations — Hagia Sophia, the Grand Bazaar, Topkapi Palace — are examined across different films and decades, revealing how Western cinema's perception of Istanbul evolved from Cold War exoticism to global modernity.

Films: From Russia with Love · Topkapi · Skyfall · Taken 2 · Inferno

04

Academic Context

This project was developed within the framework of the course Information Modeling and Web Technologies at the University of Bologna's Master's Degree in Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge.

The project engages with several core concerns of digital humanities practice: how narrative structures can organize complex cultural datasets; how interactive interfaces can support both scholarly and tourist modes of engagement; and how web technologies can be used to create experiences that are simultaneously informative, interpretive, and aesthetically compelling.

The selection of cinematic tourism as a domain reflects the growing scholarly interest in film-induced tourism and the ways that popular cinema shapes place-attachment, destination image, and cultural memory at specific locations.
